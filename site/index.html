Introduction to ClassSync

Everything academic, finally in sync.

ClassSync is an Android-first academic workspace built for students who want Google Classroom, optional Gmail reminders, manual tasks, planner views, study planning, widgets, and exam workflows in one local-first system. The product is designed to reduce scattered academic surfaces and bring deadlines, materials, and action items into a cleaner operating layer.

Why ClassSync is unique

Built like an academic operating layer, not just another task list.

Most education apps stop at one narrow workflow. ClassSync is different because it treats academic updates as a continuous flow: Classroom sync feeds local event intelligence, optional Gmail catches academic reminders, and the result lands across tasks, planners, widgets, study lanes, and exam surfaces without forcing the student to jump between disconnected tools.

Local-first by default

Academic state is kept on-device so the app can work like a personal workspace instead of a backend-heavy dashboard.

Classroom plus planner fusion

It does not just display Classroom data. It converts academic activity into planning views, task queues, reminders, and widget-ready summaries.

Optional Gmail reminder discovery

Gmail is treated as an optional academic signal layer for reminder capture rather than the center of the product.

Student workflow design

Home, tasks, planner, study planner, and exam mode are shaped around how students actually pace coursework through a semester.

Widget-aware system

The app surface extends beyond the app itself with a home screen widget that reflects the current academic pressure and focus state.

Hybrid intelligence

Rules and on-device classification work together so trusted academic patterns stay stable while messier updates still become usable signals.
